# Public Claim Boundary

Companion documentation added 2026-07-08, after publication; it does not
modify the paper. This repository's claim discipline already lives in
`docs/CLAIM_REGISTER.md` (public M24-C/M24-N register with certificate
pointers), `docs/CLAIM_CROSSWALK.md`, and
`docs/EXECUTABLE_REPLAY_SCOPE.md`. **Those files are authoritative; this
file only distills the quoting boundary.**

## Can Say

- The main meet theorem under the stated non-degenerate/terminal rules:
  Meet_weak = {24, 27} and Meet_strong = {24} (register row "main
  theorem", IDs M24-C01..C06).
- Every register row, at its registered role/status, with its certificate
  pointer (e.g. bounded Lo Shu counts 1,5,13,25,41,25,13,5,1 — finite
  proposition; the Sagrada terminal retraction with F² = F, |im F| = 12 —
  role "theorem / exact finite replay", not ID-cited in the manuscript;
  the Type-A D4/V4 subgroup/coset-tiler comparisons — finite proposition;
  the F2^4 tesseract transport counts; the 292-vertex fixed-sum polytope
  audit).
- Guardrail propositions exactly as registered — including their own
  negative scopes (e.g. the 60 = 32+12+16 terminal count-shadow carries
  "no record/profile lift or endpoint-atlas identification claim").

## Must Not Say

Verbatim from `docs/EXECUTABLE_REPLAY_SCOPE.md` ("Not Included As
Claims"):

- A universal theorem that 24 is an invariant of magic squares.
- A full Markov-basis or Hilbert-basis theorem.
- A complete Type-A/Tetra extension-tiler classification.
- A canonical Lo Shu/N-poset boundary selector.

Additionally:

- Do not promote register rows above their role/status column ("finite
  proposition", "guardrail proposition", "extension proposition" are not
  "theorem" unless the register says so).
- Do not cite development-only roadmap rows — the public register is
  deliberately reduced; what is not in it is not public.
